Most models treat a reaction time as a draw from a distribution. But the order matters: speeding up, drifting, recovering after an error. This project models the whole sequence as a dynamical system and asks what structure falls out.

## The model

LSTM and dynamical variational autoencoders learn a low-dimensional trajectory behind each participant's reaction-time sequence, without being told what to look for.

> Give the model no labels and it still separates the runs — which is exactly when a latent axis starts to feel real.

## What the latent space says

Two axes recur across participants, and they behave like different processes: one for flexibility, one for stability. The suggestion — data-driven, not assumed — is that these are distinct, and consistent with recent psychology. <EndMark />
