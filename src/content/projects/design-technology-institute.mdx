I had the privilege of working under [Constance Swaniker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constance_Swaniker) and [Bernice Gavor](https://dtiafrica.com/news/post/dti-precision-quality-internship-train-6000-youth-three-years-1800-trained-year-one-and-308-graduate-accras-inaugural-cohort) at DTI and A&A. Constance is an incredible woman, she took her studio and turned it into the driving force behind a national vocational training school, and now she's scaling it across a country where young people struggle to find rigorous training to actually get them work-ready. Bernice runs the Precision Quality internship that's at the center of that effort, a program aiming to train 6,000 youth over three years, with 1,800 trained and 308 graduating from Accra's inaugural cohort in year one alone. DTI is also the first institution in Africa to receive accreditation through the American Welding Society, which says a lot about how seriously they take the "rigorous" part.

<Figure src="/gallery/design-technology-institute/constance-bernice.jpg" align="right" caption="With Constance Swaniker and Bernice Gavor in Accra." />

My part was mostly technical. I built out the infrastructure to collect, analyze, and securely store survey data from 15,000 students across six campuses, designed the questionnaires, built the pipeline end to end, and worked with faculty and staff so they could keep running it after I left.

<Figure src="/gallery/design-technology-institute/cohort-group.jpg" align="left" caption="With the A&A team at the 25th-anniversary celebration." />

Threw in a couple lectures too, one on AI and one on personal and professional organization, because apparently I have opinions on that.

<Figure src="/gallery/design-technology-institute/anniversary-event.jpg" align="right" caption="The 25th-anniversary gathering in Accra." />

Wonderful summer. If you're ever in West Africa, go say hi to DTI and A&A. <EndMark />
