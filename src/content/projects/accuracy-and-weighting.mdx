Nonprobability panels are the workhorse of modern polling: cheaper, faster, and — the claim goes — accurate enough once you weight them back to the population. This project takes that claim at its word and checks it against ground truth, year after year, for the better part of two decades.

## The setup

Seventeen years of YouGov Cooperative Election Study data, run through every major weighting scheme and compared against validated election outcomes. The pipeline harmonizes fifteen sources into one comparable frame, so the only thing changing between estimates is the method.

<Display>Weighting can fix a biased sample. It cannot manufacture the people who were never in it.</Display>

## What the record shows

Averaged across years and methods, error in election estimates exceeds seven percent — and, tellingly, it does not shrink over time. Better weights help at the margins; they do not close the gap. <EndMark />
